# Methods Limitations

This study is subject to several important limitations, which are intrinsic to its scope and objectives.

## 1. Data Representation Constraints

All analyses are performed using CMS Open Data at the NanoAOD tier. This format excludes lower-level reconstruction information, such as particle-flow candidates and detailed detector response components. As a result:
- MET cannot be recomputed from first principles.
- Nuisance models act phenomenologically on reconstructed quantities.

The conclusions of this work are therefore conditional on the information content of NanoAOD and do not apply to internal CMS analyses with access to full reconstruction and calibration data.

## 2. Simplified Nuisance Models

The nuisance model families considered are intentionally low-dimensional and do not correspond to official CMS systematic uncertainty variations. They are not intended to represent a complete detector model.

Their purpose is comparative: to test whether distinct, physically motivated hypotheses can be distinguished given the available observables. The study does not claim that these models exhaust the space of plausible detector effects.

## 3. No Detector Performance Claims

This work does not measure detector performance, resolution, or calibration quality. Agreement or disagreement between models and data should not be interpreted as evidence for or against the adequacy of CMS reconstruction algorithms.

## 4. No Physics Discovery Sensitivity

The analysis is not optimized for sensitivity to beyond-the-Standard-Model signals and makes no discovery claims. All conclusions are methodological, concerning inference and identifiability under reduced observability.

## 5. Dependence on Chosen Observables

Identifiability is evaluated with respect to a specific set of observables and stratifications, preregistered in advance. It is possible that alternative observables, unavailable at the NanoAOD tier, could break degeneracies observed here.

This does not invalidate the results; rather, it characterizes the representational limits of the public data format.

## 6. Statistical Power Limitations

While minimum yield thresholds are enforced, some stratified tests may still be statistically limited in extreme regions of phase space. Such bins are excluded conservatively, which may reduce sensitivity to subtle effects.

## 7. External Validity

The findings apply to the specific dataset, selection, and reconstruction era studied (CMS 2016). Extension to other eras or experiments would require repetition of the analysis under comparable conditions.
