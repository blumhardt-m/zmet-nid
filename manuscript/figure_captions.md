# Figures

![Inclusive MET comparison (Fig. 1)](figures/inclusive_fit/met_familyA_vs_familyB.png)

**Fig. 1.** Inclusive missing transverse momentum distributions for 233,524 Z→μ⁺μ⁻
candidate events selected from CMS Open Data (DoubleMuon 2016). The target
distribution (black) is constructed by applying the recoil-response nuisance
family (Family A) at the working point α = 0.05, corresponding to a 5% MET
scale distortion growing linearly with min(pT(Z), 100 GeV)/100. Family A
(dashed red) is shown at its best-fit parameter α = 0.050, which trivially
recovers the target. Family B (dotted blue) is the topology-mixture nuisance
family fitted by grid search to the same target, with best-fit β = +0.290.
The lower panel shows the ratio of each model to the target. The topology-mixture
model provides an acceptable description of the inclusive MET distribution
(χ²(B) = 3.13 for 11 degrees of freedom, p = 0.989). Both models are fitted
to the inclusive distribution; no per-stratum refitting is performed.

![pT(Z)-stratified MET comparison (Fig. 2)](figures/stratified_tests/met_familyA_vs_familyB_by_zpt.png)

**Fig. 2.** Missing transverse momentum distributions in four pT(Z) strata for
233,524 selected Z→μ⁺μ⁻ events. The four bins follow the protocol definition:
pT(Z) < 10 GeV (N = 92,608), 10 ≤ pT(Z) < 30 GeV (N = 89,387),
30 ≤ pT(Z) < 60 GeV (N = 34,312), and pT(Z) ≥ 60 GeV (N = 17,217). The
target (black), Family A (dashed red), and Family B (dotted) are evaluated using
the parameter values obtained from the inclusive fit only; no per-stratum
refitting is applied. The per-stratum discrimination is quantified by
Δχ² = χ²(B) − χ²(A). The discrepancy between the models grows with recoil
activity, reaching Δχ² = 1,432 in the pT(Z) ≥ 60 GeV bin, compared with
χ²(B) = 3.13 on the inclusive distribution. Family B is systematically
elevated relative to the target at low MET values in the highest pT(Z) bin,
reflecting its inability to reproduce the recoil-correlated MET shift of
Family A when conditioned on pT(Z).

![Jet-multiplicity-stratified MET comparison (Fig. 3)](figures/stratified_tests/met_familyA_vs_familyB_by_njets.png)

**Fig. 3.** Missing transverse momentum distributions stratified by jet
multiplicity for 233,524 selected Z→μ⁺μ⁻ events. Jets are required to satisfy
pT > 30 GeV and are cleaned against both Z-candidate muons using ΔR > 0.4.
The three categories are: 0-jet (N = 171,385), 1-jet (N = 44,706), and
≥2-jet (N = 17,433). The target (black), Family A (dashed red), and Family B
(dotted) are evaluated at the inclusive best-fit parameters without refitting.
Family B assigns global event weights of approximately 0.91, 1.17, and 1.44 to
the 0-jet, 1-jet, and ≥2-jet categories respectively at β = +0.290, producing
a 44% event-count inflation in the ≥2-jet stratum relative to the target.
This population inconsistency yields Δχ² = 3,415 in the ≥2-jet bin, the
largest per-stratum discrimination observed in this study. Family A achieves
χ²(A) ≈ 0 in all three bins.
